Frequently Asked Questions

What is waste segregation?

Waste segregation is the process of separating different types of waste at the source. This helps in effective recycling, reduces landfill waste, and makes waste management more efficient.

How can I reduce my household waste?

You can reduce household waste by:

  • Composting organic waste
  • Buying products with minimal packaging
  • Reusing and recycling items
  • Avoiding single-use plastics

What is the difference between dry and wet waste?

Dry waste includes recyclable materials like paper, plastic, glass, and metal. Wet waste consists of organic materials like food scraps, kitchen waste, and garden waste that can be composted.

How often should I schedule waste pickup?

Waste pickup frequency depends on your local municipality guidelines. Typically, dry waste can be collected weekly, while wet waste should be disposed of more frequently to prevent odor and pest issues.

What items should not be thrown in regular trash?

Hazardous items like:

  • Electronic waste
  • Batteries
  • Chemical products
  • Medical waste
Should be disposed of through special collection programs.
